This tutorial walks through a complete research session using Claude and Atlas MCP. By the end, you'll have a structured picture of civic work in a city, including who's active, what sources back them, and where the gaps are.

**Prerequisites:**
- Atlas account with Pro or Team plan
- Claude Desktop or Claude Code connected to Atlas ([setup guide](/mcp/overview))

## Step 1: Start with a broad question

Open Claude and ask about the city you're researching:

> **You:** Who's working on housing and labor issues in Kansas City?

Claude will use Atlas's `search_entities` tool to find matching entities and return a summary. You might see something like:

> **Claude:** I found 31 entities related to housing and labor in Kansas City. Here are the most prominent:
>
> 1. **KC Tenants** (organization) — Tenant organizing collective fighting for housing justice. Sources: Kansas City Star, KCUR
> 2. **Stand Up KC** (campaign) — Fight for $15 minimum wage campaign. Sources: The Pitch KC, KSHB
> 3. **Habitat for Humanity KC** (organization) — Affordable housing construction. Sources: Kansas City Business Journal
> ...

## Step 2: Drill into specifics

Pick an entity that interests you and ask for details:

> **You:** Tell me more about KC Tenants. What are all the sources?

Claude will use `get_entity` and `get_entity_sources` to retrieve the full record:

> **Claude:** **KC Tenants** is a citywide tenant organizing collective in Kansas City, MO focused on housing justice and tenant rights.
>
> **Sources (4):**
> 1. "KC Tenants escalates fight against corporate landlords" — *Kansas City Star*, March 2026
> 2. "Inside Kansas City's tenant organizing movement" — *KCUR*, January 2026
> 3. "Housing advocates push for rent stabilization" — *The Pitch KC*, December 2025
> 4. "Tenant union wins first eviction moratorium" — *Flatland KC*, November 2025

Notice that every claim is backed by specific, dated, verifiable sources.

## Step 3: Check coverage gaps

Ask what Atlas *doesn't* know about:

> **You:** What issue areas have low coverage in Kansas City?

Claude will use `get_place_coverage` to analyze gaps:

> **Claude:** Kansas City coverage analysis:
>
> **Strong coverage:** Housing affordability (18 entities), labor and worker power (9 entities), local government (7 entities)
>
> **Limited coverage:** Environmental justice (2 entities), broadband access (1 entity), restorative justice (0 entities), addiction and harm reduction (1 entity)
>
> The gaps may indicate areas where Atlas needs more discovery, or where fewer organizations are publicly active.

## Step 4: Explore the broader context

Ask about the city's profile and issue landscape:

> **You:** What's the socioeconomic profile of Kansas City? What are the main civic issues?

Claude will use `get_place_profile` and `get_place_issue_signals` to provide context.

## Step 5: Follow threads

Use what you've learned to go deeper:

> **You:** Find organizations working on environmental justice in the broader Kansas City metro area

> **You:** Are there any worker cooperatives in Kansas City?

> **You:** What other entities are connected to Stand Up KC?

Each question gives Claude a new angle to query Atlas, building up a layered picture of civic work in the city.

## What you've accomplished

In a single conversation, you've:
- Identified 30+ people and organizations working on your issues
- Verified entries against specific, dated sources
- Discovered coverage gaps that might inform further research
- Built a contextual understanding of the city's civic landscape

All without opening a browser tab, scrolling through search results, or maintaining a spreadsheet.
