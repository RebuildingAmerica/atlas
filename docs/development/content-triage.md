# Content Triage

[Docs](../README.md) > [Development](./README.md) > Content Triage

Atlas is not just a software project. It is also a source-linked
directory. That means discovery results need human judgment.

This guide explains how to review, edit, merge, and discard entries so
the directory stays useful and trustworthy.

## What We Are Trying To Protect

Atlas should surface:

- Specific people, organizations, initiatives, campaigns, and events
- Clear ties to place and issue area
- Strong source attribution
- Entries that are useful for research, sourcing, and public discovery

We do not want a directory full of vague names, thin listings, or weakly
sourced placeholders.

## What Counts As A Strong Entry

A strong Atlas entry is:

- Specific
- Source-linked
- Grounded in a real place
- Clearly tied to issue areas
- Useful to someone trying to understand who is doing what

Strong entries usually describe real work, not just institutional
existence.

## Confirm, Edit, Merge, Or Discard

### Confirm

Confirm an entry when:

- The person or organization is clearly real
- The source support is strong enough
- The issue-area and place data make sense
- The entry is already useful as written

### Edit

Edit an entry when:

- The core lead is good, but the wording is weak
- The issue-area tags need adjustment
- The place information needs clarification
- The summary can be made more specific or more readable

### Merge

Merge when multiple entries clearly refer to the same underlying person,
organization, or initiative.

When merging:

- Keep the strongest name and summary
- Preserve the source links
- Do not lose meaningful detail from the merged records
- Be conservative if identity is not actually clear

### Discard

Discard when:

- The entry is too vague to be useful
- The source support is weak or misleading
- The result is a duplicate with no added value
- The entry does not really represent Atlas’s target subject matter

## Ambiguous Cases

Be careful with:

- Same-name entities in different places
- Broad organizations with only generic local chapter references
- Entries that mention an issue without showing meaningful work
- Sources that repeat the same weak information without adding confidence

If you are unsure, prefer editing or holding for review over forcing a
confident merge.

## Source Attribution And Confidence

Source links are part of the product, not just backup evidence.

Ask:

- Does the source support the actual claim in the entry?
- Is the source local, detailed, and specific?
- Are multiple sources reinforcing confidence, or just echoing the same
  thin claim?

Higher confidence usually comes from:

- local reporting
- detailed profiles
- organizational pages with substantive program detail
- multiple independent mentions

## Triage Heuristics

Good triage questions:

- Would this help a researcher, organizer, journalist, or member of the
  public understand who is doing real work here?
- Is this entry specific enough to be worth following up on?
- If this were the only result someone saw, would it feel trustworthy?

If the answer is no, the entry probably needs work or should not be kept.
